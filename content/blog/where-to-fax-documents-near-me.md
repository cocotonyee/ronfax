## FedEx Office (formerly FedEx Kinkos)

FedEx Office locations offer self‑service and staff‑assisted faxing at many branches. You typically pay **per page** for local and long‑distance faxes, and pricing may differ by store. Expect to wait during lunch hours or near business districts.

**Pros:** Wide footprint, staff help if you are unsure.  
**Cons:** You must travel, possibly pay parking, and stand in line. Document handling is visible in a public area.

## Staples, UPS Store, and similar shipping centers

Staples and The UPS Store franchises commonly advertise fax services alongside printing and shipping. Like FedEx, you usually pay **per page** with possible minimums. Hours follow retail—not ideal if you work late or need to send something on a Sunday.

**Pros:** One stop if you also need printing.  
**Cons:** Inconsistent pricing between franchises; same privacy and travel drawbacks as FedEx.

## Libraries and community centers

Some public libraries offer fax access or referrals. Availability is **highly local**: urban systems may have better equipment; rural branches may not offer outbound fax at all. Never count on a library without calling ahead.

**Pros:** Sometimes lower cost for residents.  
**Cons:** Limited hours, long waits, and policies that change with funding.
