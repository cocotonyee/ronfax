## How a "fax from printer" is supposed to work

Most business-class printers combine **scan + print + fax**. If an analog phone line is plugged into the correct port and the line supports fax tones, you can:

1. Place the document on the flatbed or ADF  
2. Enter the destination fax number on the panel  
3. Press *Send* or *Start*

If that sounds simple, reality is messier: wrong jacks, VoIP adapters that clip fax signals, and drivers that quietly fail without an obvious error.

## When your printer cannot fax

You might see failures if:

- Your home uses **VoIP-only** voice (many fiber and cable bundles)  
- The **RJ-11** line was never connected or was removed  
- The MFP's fax license expired (common on leased office copiers)  
- You are mobile and the printer is two towns away

When any of these happen, stop fighting hardware. You only need a **clear PDF of each page**.

## What to do if you don't have a printer or working fax line

### Option A — Scan with your phone

Modern iPhones and Android phones produce sharp PDFs. Lay the page on a flat surface, use good light, and export a single multi-page PDF when possible.

### Option B — Use a file already on your computer

If the document originated as PDF or Word, export or print to PDF. That file is ready for online fax.
