Your phone already has everything you need: a camera for scanning-style photos, Files or Photos for PDFs, and a browser. The missing piece is a **fax line**—and that is what an online fax service replaces. RonFax is built so you never have to clutter your phone with another **fax app** that wants a subscription.

## Why skip the fax app?

**Less friction** — No account harvest, no yearly plan upsell inside the App Store funnel. Open the site, upload, send.

**Privacy signal** — Many fax apps aggressively request contacts and notifications. A browser workflow keeps you one tap away from your document and checkout only.

**Same network** — Retail fax counters and glossy apps alike ultimately dial a fax modem. RonFax routes your file over the telephone network once payment is verified—your phone never needs its own modem.

## Fax from iPhone (Safari) in plain steps

1. **Prepare the file** — Export or save your document as **PDF**, or take a straight photo if you only have a paper copy. Trim edges in Photos if helpful.
2. **Open RonFax** — Use Safari and go to the send page on ronfax.com.
3. **Enter the fax number** — Ten-digit US destination (RonFax uses +1; you type the domestic number).
4. **Upload** — Choose your PDF or images. Multiple files merge into one transmission.
5. **Pay once** — Complete secure checkout (card or wallets your device supports). You land on a **status** URL you can bookmark or revisit from email.

Works the same way on cellular data or Wi-Fi as long as the upload completes.

## Fax from Android (Chrome)

Chrome on Android handles file pickers reliably for PDF and images. If your bank or clinic emailed a PDF attachment, save it with **Downloads** or **Drive**, then attach it on the RonFax form. Giant mail threads are unnecessary—**one PDF, one fax number**.

## Practical tips that prevent retries

**Resolution** — Fax is not glossy print. Crisp dark text on white beats colorful photos of screens.

**File type** — Word docs should be exported to PDF before upload; RonFax will prompt you similarly if you try something unsupported.

**Number check** — Confirm the recipient’s fax with their office or an official portal. Busy signals and wrong numbers still happen on the phone network.
